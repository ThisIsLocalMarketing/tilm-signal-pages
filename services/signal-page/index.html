Approval-first workflow

From main action to approved page.

A short fit-and-proof path keeps the page focused, reviewable, and connected only after approval.

1
Pick one main action.

Call, book, order, request, review, read details, or get directions — the page should not become a mini website.

2
Collect confirmed facts, links, images, and approved wording.

Use owner-approved business facts and supplied assets; light cleanup is included, broad branding is not.

3
Draft the page for owner review.

Basic includes 1 proof/revision pass; Custom includes up to 3 proof/revision passes.

4
Publish/connect only after approval.

After approval, TILM publishes or connects the page at the agreed public URL.

5
Track only if infrastructure is ready.

Do not promise tracking unless the QR/analytics setup is actually ready.

Product fit notes
Best for
  • One simple destination after a QR scan, card click, post, text, or ad.
  • A scattered website/link path that needs one clear customer action.
  • Local card, QR, or social campaigns that need somewhere approved to send people.
Scope clarity
  • Basic = one page, one CTA, up to 5 supporting buttons.
  • Custom = multiple sections, links/buttons, and pages if needed, with stronger copy/layout and custom visual treatment.
  • Updates after approval are separate unless correcting an error from the approved proof.
Visual direction
  • Phone-first preview and CTA hierarchy.
  • Offer / Place / Proof / Action structure.
  • Tracking language stays conservative: scans, visits, tracked action clicks, scan-to-action rate only when enabled.
